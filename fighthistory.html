UFC229: Khabib Wins Via Submission (neck crank), Round 4.

The Ultimate Fighting Championship (UFC) organised the mixed martial arts event UFC 229: Khabib vs. McGregor , which took place on October 6, 2018, at the
T-Mobile Arena in Paradise, Nevada. Nurmagomedov entered the bout with a perfect 26-0 record, while McGregor was the first UFC fighter to simultaneously hold titles
in two divisions. The president of the organisation, Dana White, referred to the match as "the biggest in UFC history." Following the bout, Nurmagomedov exited the
octagon and charged Dillon Danis, McGregor's cornerman. A fight erupted between McGregor and Abubakar Nurmagomedov (Khabib's cousin) as they attempted
to exit the octagon shortly after. Zubaira Tukhugov and Esed Emiragaev, two of Nurmagomedov's cornermen, then attacked McGregor inside the octagon. The
Nevada State Athletic Commission (NSAC) delayed Nurmagomedov's paycheck for the bout as a result of the incident while it looked into his behaviour. At UFC Fight
Night: Volkan vs Smith on October 27, Artem Lobov, a teammate of McGregor's who was confronted by Nurmagomedov in April 2018, was to face Tukhugov. During the
post-fight interview, Nurmagomedov apologised to the NSAC and said that the trash-talk directed at his family, country, and religion by the McGregor team as well as
the UFC 223 bus incident were the causes of his outburst. Eventually they were both fined for there roles in the incident by the The Nevada State Athletic Commission.

UFC223: Khabib Wins Via Decision (unanimous).

On April 7, 2018, the Barclays Centre in Brooklyn, New York, played host to the Ultimate Fighting Championship's mixed martial arts event, UFC 223:
Khabib vs. Iaquinta. Following the media day for the event on April 5, Conor McGregor and his group ambushed a bus carrying a number of fighters
were supposed to compete there as it was leaving the arena after a press conference. In that bus were Nurmagomedov and two teammates. During the
attack, items were thrown through a glass, shattering it. This action caused multiple changes to the event, including the following: The fight between
Artem Lobov and Alex Caceres was cancelled after Lobov was kicked out of the event for having a direct hand in the incident. Michael Chiesa, the
lightweight champion of The Ultimate Fighter 15, was set to compete against Anthony Pettis, a former WEC and UFC Lightweight Champion, but he
withdrew from the fight after suffering many cuts from a broken window. Seven weeks ago, at UFC Fight Night: Cowboy vs. Medeiros, a flyweight
matchup between former UFC Flyweight Championship contender Ray Borg and Brandon Moreno was scheduled to take place. However, the pairing
was postponed and rescheduled to occur at this event because Moreno suffered a minor injury. Additionally, this match was called off after Borg
experienced problems with glass shards in his eye. In the end, McGregor surrendered to the NYPD and was detained while charges were being brought.
He was eventually charged with three charges of assault and one count of criminal mischief and arrested alongside his SBG teammate Cian Cowley.
With bail amounts of $50,000 and $25,000, respectively, they were both freed. After McGregor entered a no contest plea to a count of disorderly conduct
for his participation in the brawl and completed five days of community service, the charges were dismissed in late July.
